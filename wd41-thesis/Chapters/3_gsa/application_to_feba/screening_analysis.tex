\subsection{Screening Analysis}\label{sub:sa_application_screening_analysis}

% Introductory paragraph
A screening analysis to identify noninfluential parameters was first carried out on the $27$ model parameters using the Morris screening method.
The \gls[hyper=false]{qoi} for this screening analysis was the time-averaged quantities for all output types (cladding temperature, channel pressure drop and liquid carryover) as explained in Sec.~\ref{sub:sa_application_simulation_experiment}.

A more important finding here is that the non-influential parameters identified by both variants of the Morris method are confirmed by the total-effect indices based on the variance decomposition.
The estimations of these indices were obtained with small uncertainty.

% Consistency of results
To check the consistency of the screening, Fig.~\ref{fig:ch3_plot_influential_noninfluential_runs} illustrates the notion of noninfluential and influential parameters,
in terms of the effects of their perturbations on the transient of three different outputs.
\gls[hyper=false]{trace} was executed using $500$ \gls[hyper=false]{mc} samples of parameter value from each of the sets.
The figure confirms that the use of time-averaged quantity for each output types as viable \gls[hyper=false]{qoi} for screening as the identified noninfluential parameters were indeed the ones that result in minor distortion of all outputs transient as compared to the distortion brought by the influential parameter perturbations.
Furthermore, it also confirms that by making the union set of all the important parameters subsets (each with respect to different outputs), the noninfluential parameters are indeed valid for all outputs.
